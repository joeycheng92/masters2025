AUGUSTA, Ga. -- The pristine greens of Augusta National weren't the only place witnessing drama on Thursday. The opening round of the highly anticipated Fantasy Masters produced fireworks, shockers, and head-scratching moments, leaving the leaderboard tightly packed at the top but littered with casualties further down.

Veteran Justin Rose (-7) showed flashes of his major-winning pedigree, navigating the virtual course with precision to grab a share of the early lead. His stellar performance provided a massive boost for his fantasy backers (none of whom are in this league). Joey Cheng, whose strategic of major champions have anchored his own surge to the top spot, also at -7.

However, Cheng, along with second-place manager Bill Jin (-6), might be feeling the pressure already. While their leading men delivered, whispers around the fantasy clubhouse suggest the rest of their rosters are bleeding strokes.

The biggest story, however, wasn't at the top, but centered around the stunning collapse of Rory McIlroy. Expected by many to contend, McIlroy's performance was nothing short of disastrous. Plagued by unforced errors and questionable course management, his round imploded spectacularly, leaving his fantasy managers reeling. Sources described the performance with brutal honesty, comparing the meltdown unfavorably to infamous sporting chokes ‚Äì a truly forgettable day for the Northern Irishman's backers.

Adding to the day's intrigue were the unexpected absences of Ryan Fox and Colin Roche. Both were conspicuously missing from the tee sheets, with unofficial reports citing a bizarre logistical snafu involving "an excessive number of bags misplaced somewhere in Miami." Their fantasy managers were left scrambling for emergency replacements, their tournament hopes potentially derailed before they even began.

Elsewhere, Kevin Yu provided a surprising bright spot for his manager, carding a +4 that significantly "defied expectations." While over par, the score was far better than pundits predicted, representing a small victory on a tough scoring day.

Ben Wedd, meanwhile, embodied consistency, grinding out a respectable +1, keeping himself firmly in the mix without any major fireworks.

Not everyone navigated the day smoothly. Jeremy Shen endured a turbulent round marred by what sources described as "multiple admin issues seemingly caused by Jock." Jack struggled to a +4, while Jock soared with a -4, putting them eight shots apart and demonstrating that even twins can have wildly different days at Augusta.

As the (digital) sun sets on Day 1, the stage is set for a compelling second round. Can Rose maintain his form? Will Cheng and Jin get support from their struggling rosters? Can McIlroy's backers recover? And will Fox and Roche ever locate their luggage? Friday promises more drama at the Fantasy Masters.
